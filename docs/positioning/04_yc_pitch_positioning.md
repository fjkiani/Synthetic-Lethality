## One More Thing

The most important trial in the brain cancer graveyard is ACT IV — Celldex's rindopepimut vaccine trial. It enrolled 745 patients, cost $200M+, and failed completely. The post-hoc analysis revealed why: the tumor simply stopped expressing the antigen the vaccine was targeting. The immune pressure selected for antigen-negative cells. The target disappeared.

This is called immunoediting. It is the fundamental reason why every antigen-targeted brain cancer therapy — vaccines, CAR-T cells, antibody-drug conjugates — faces a structural ceiling. The tumor can always lose the antigen.

CrisPRO's engine does not target antigens. It targets structural dependencies — biological requirements that are created by the tumor's own molecular state and cannot be edited away. When a lung cancer cell activates the brain-colonization program (by expressing ZEB1), it becomes structurally dependent on αV integrin. The tumor cannot escape that dependency by silencing a surface protein. If it tries to escape by turning off ZEB1, it loses the very program that made it brain-tropic in the first place.

ACT IV proved that antigen targeting fails. Our engine is built on the lesson.

**Synthetic lethal dependencies cannot be immunoedited away. That is the paradigm shift.**
